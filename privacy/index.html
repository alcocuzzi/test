Privacy policy

INTRODUCTION

This Privacy Policy explains how Daniela Cocuzzi Professional Organizer (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and protects your personal data when you visit or interact with our website: https://www.danielacocuzziorganizer.ie


By using this website or providing your personal information, you confirm that you are at least 13 years of age. We are committed to safeguarding your privacy and handling your personal data transparently and responsibly, in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).


WHO WE ARE

Legal business name: Daniela Cocuzzi Professional Organizer
Email: [email protected]


We are the data controller for the purposes of data protection legislation, meaning we determine how and why your personal data is processed. Please ensure that the personal information you provide is accurate and up to date. You may notify us of any changes by contacting us at the email address above.


PERSONAL DATA WE COLLECT AND WHY

“Personal data” means any information that can identify you directly or indirectly. We may collect and process the following categories of data:


a) Communication Information

This includes any messages you send to us via:

  • Contact forms

  • Email

  • Phone, text, or messaging apps

  • Social media platforms


Purpose:
To respond to enquiries, manage communication, maintain records, and address any legal matters if necessary.


Legal basis:
Legitimate interests — to operate our business and respond appropriately to communications.


b) Client and Service Information

This may include:

  • Name

  • Contact details

  • Address

  • Service details

  • Payment-related information (processed securely by third-party providers)


Purpose:
To provide organizing services, manage bookings, process payments, and maintain transaction records.


Legal basis:
Performance of a contract or steps taken at your request prior to entering a contract.


c) Website Usage Information

This includes information about how you interact with our website, such as pages visited or content viewed.


Purpose:
To operate and improve our website, ensure security, and deliver relevant content.


Legal basis:
Legitimate interests — to manage and improve our online presence.


d) Technical Information

This may include:

  • IP address

  • Browser type and version

  • Device information

  • Time zone and usage data


This information is collected through analytics tools.


Purpose:
To analyze website performance, improve user experience, and protect our website.


Legal basis:
Legitimate interests — business administration and growth.


e) Marketing Preferences

This includes your preferences regarding receiving updates or marketing communications from us.


Purpose:
To send relevant information, promotions, or updates (where permitted).


Legal basis:
Consent or legitimate interests, depending on the circumstances.


SENSITIVE PERSONAL DATA

We do not collect or process special category data, such as:

  • Health information

  • Religious or philosophical beliefs

  • Political opinions

  • Biometric or genetic data

  • Criminal records


FAILURE TO PROVIDE PERSONAL DATA

If you do not provide personal data that is necessary for us to deliver a service, we may be unable to proceed with that service. If this occurs, we will inform you accordingly.


HOW WE COLLECT YOUR DATA

We collect personal data in the following ways:

  • Directly from you when you contact us or use our services

  • Automatically through cookies and analytics tools when you use our website

  • From trusted third parties such as analytics providers, payment processors, or social media platforms

  • From publicly available sources, where lawful


MARKETING COMMUNICATIONS

We may contact you with marketing communications if:

  • You have requested information or services from us, or

  • You have given explicit consent


You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by:

  • Using the unsubscribe link in emails, or

  • Contacting us at [email protected]

Opting out of marketing does not affect communications related to existing services or legal obligations.


SHARING YOUR PERSONAL DATA

We may share your data with trusted third parties, including:

  • IT and website service providers

  • Professional advisers (such as accountants, legal advisers, or insurers)

  • Regulatory or governmental authorities where legally required

  • Business partners in the event of a sale, transfer, or restructuring

All third parties are required to protect your data and use it only for specified purposes.


INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

Your personal data may be transferred outside the UK or EEA where necessary. When this happens, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as:

  • Transfers to countries approved by relevant authorities

  • Use of approved contractual clauses or frameworks

  • Explicit consent where required


DATA SECURITY

We take appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or disclosure. Access to your data is limited to individuals who need it for legitimate business purposes and who are subject to confidentiality obligations.


In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities where legally required.


DATA RETENTION

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.


For example:

  • Client and transaction data may be retained for up to six years to comply with tax and legal requirements. In some cases, we may anonymize data for statistical or research purposes.


YOUR LEGAL RIGHTS

Under data protection laws, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data

  • Request correction or deletion

  • Restrict or object to processing

  • Request data portability

  • Withdraw consent (where applicable)


To exercise your rights, please contact us at: [email protected]


We aim to respond within one month.


COMPLAINTS

If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with:

  • Your local data protection authority (EU), or

  • The Information Commissioner’s Office (UK)


We encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concerns directly.


THIRD-PARTY LINKS

Our website may contain links to external websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those websites. Please review their privacy policies separately.


COOKIES
Our website uses cookies to improve functionality and user experience. You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings.
